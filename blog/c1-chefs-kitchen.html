My Thoughts So Far

Sunday, May 24th 2015

For the past few years I've lived abroad and tried my best to immerse myself in the local cultures. So, to now finally be back stateside in my home country, I find it a little funny that I am again presented with the challenge of immersion. But, I think that's the part of the DBC experience that attracted me the most. I want an experience that is immersive, challenging, and takes you out of your comfort zone. I think that I operate best in these types of situations, and after living abroad, being out of my comfort zone on a daily basis, I feel that I am going to NEED that excitement of being challenged and being pushed to really enjoy what I do. I know that at times I will be nervous, scared, lost, confused, etc - but at the same time happy that I have pushed myself to this point, and am surrounded by others who also like the challenge.

After now finishing week 1 and learning more about the environment of DBC and the culture of DBC, my expectations haven't changed, but I do feel more confident in my choice to attend. What set DBC apart for me was the focus on culture, and the focus on an individual as a human being, and not just a developer. I don't want a program that will simply improve my development abilities. I want a program that will develop me. As it was mentioned in the video, this should be lived as an experience, not a product or service - and I think all good experiences have you grow and improve as a person.

With all of the confidence I have in myself and in DBC, I am still nervous of course. I am 100 percent excited to be in this type of learning environment, and feel this type of environment is where I learn best. But, the intensity of the program does have me intimidated. I am used to being thrown head first into things that are completely foreign to me - and at the same time I'm human, and I think it's only normal to have that "what am I getting myself into" moment -but I know I am not the only one feeling this way - and can already tell that my cohort will band together to pull eachother through so that we can all make sense of this immersive experience.
