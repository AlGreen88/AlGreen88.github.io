Conflicts

Working Through Disagreements

July 19th, 2015

Having to write about conflicts is a little hard for me - yes I've had them, and I do think they are unavoidable at times - but it's just in my nature to avoid conflicts if at all possible. I don't think this is exactly the healthiest approach to resolving a disagreement, but it has worked for the most part for me.

A recent conflict arose when I decided to make the move back to the United States to attend DBC. My girlfriend, who would have to stay in Taiwan was at first not exactly excited at the idea of a long distance relationship(honestly, I wasn't either). Throughout the conflict I felt a whirlwind of emotions - was this the right choice? Could we make it? Is it worth the risk? - To make matters worse I had to try and handle my own reservations as well as hers.

To really get through it, we had to have a sit down and serious conversation. We put what we expected and wanted all out on the table - and we both walked away feeling confident about our relationship and what we could accompish as long as we are both putting in the effort to make it work.

I don't think I would change how we handled the conflict - we were able to resolve it with no harm done, and ended up stronger from it. I learned that if you can just take the time to sit down and talk through something, you're more than likely going to have positive results.
